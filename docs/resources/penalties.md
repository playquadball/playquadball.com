# Common Penalties Reference

Quick reference guide for the most common penalties in quadball.

## Penalty Types

### Back-to-Hoops
**Duration:** None (procedural reset)
**Effect:** Player must return to hoops and reset before rejoining play

Common situations:
- Minor illegal contact
- Interposition interference (minor)
- Immunity violations
- Illegal pursuit of flag

### Blue Card  
**Duration:** 1 minute in penalty box
**Effect:** Team plays short-handed

Common situations:
- Illegal substitution
- Delay of game
- Playing while off stick
- Sideline infractions
- Illegal circumvention

### Yellow Card
**Duration:** 1 minute in penalty box  
**Effect:** Team plays short-handed

Common situations:
- Contact from behind
- Illegal contact (standard)
- Unsportsmanlike conduct
- Feigning injury
- Disrespect to officials

### Red Card
**Duration:** Ejection from game
**Effect:** Permanent player removal

Common situations:
- Intentional contact to head/neck
- Tackling a helpless receiver
- Egregious unsportsmanlike conduct
- Fighting or violent conduct
- Intentional goaltending by non-keeper

## Contact Penalties Quick Reference

| Situation | Legal Contact | Penalty |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Chaser tackles chaser with ball | From front only | Legal / Yellow if from behind |
| Chaser tackles chaser without ball | Never legal | Yellow Card |
| Contact with helpless receiver | Never legal | Yellow/Red Card |  
| Beater-beater contact | Limited contact only | Yellow Card if excessive |
| Any contact to head/neck area | Never legal | Red Card |

## Procedural Penalties

| Violation | Penalty | Notes |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| False start | Blue Card | Moving before whistle |
| Illegal substitution | Blue Card | Entering play incorrectly |
| Too many players | Yellow Card (Captain) | Illegal set of players |
| Delay of game | Blue Card + Turnover | Stalling with ball |
| Illegal reset | Turnover | Second backward pass |

## Penalty Box Rules

- **Entering:** Must go immediately when carded
- **Serving time:** Starts when player sits in box
- **Early release:** Goals by non-offending team can release players
- **Multiple penalties:** Served consecutively, not simultaneously
- **Keeper penalties:** Must switch headband before going to box

## Appeal Process

- **During game:** Only speaking captain may discuss calls with Head Referee
- **After explanation:** Head Referee's decision is final
- **Post-game:** Rules clarifications only, not judgment calls
- **Tournament disputes:** Escalated to Tournament Director for non-officiating matters
