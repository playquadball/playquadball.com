# Chaser & Keeper Guide

### **The Basics: Your Role and Gear**

As a **Chaser** (white headband) or **Keeper** (green headband), your primary job is to score goals with the volleyball and defend your hoops. You are the core of your team's offense and defense.

**Your Equipment:**

- **Headband:** White for Chasers, Green for the Keeper. Must be worn on your forehead.
- **Stick:** Must be held between your legs ("mounted") to participate in play. If you're knocked off your stick, you're out of the play until you go back to your hoops.
- **Mouthguard:** Mandatory for your safety.
- **Team Jersey:** Must have a unique number (0-99).

**Core Responsibilities:**

- **Offense:** Work with your teammates to move the volleyball down the field and throw it through the opponent's hoops for 10 points.
- **Defense:** Prevent the opposing team from scoring by tackling, blocking, and intercepting the volleyball.
- **Communication:** Constantly talk to your beaters and fellow chasers about offensive plays and defensive threats.

---

### **On-Field Strategy & Positioning**

**Offensive Play ("Drive"):**

- **Initiating a Drive:** Your team is on offense when you gain possession of the volleyball.
- **Advancing the Ball:** You must move the ball forward. You can't just hang back in your own half. You're expected to advance past midfield at a walking pace or faster. Stalling or intentionally delaying the game can result in a turnover (**Delay of Game** - Blue Card + Turnover).
- **Resetting:** You can carry or pass the volleyball backward across midfield *once* per drive. This is called a "reset." A second reset is an illegal turnover. You cannot throw the ball backward across a line unless it's a legitimate pass attempt to a teammate.
- **Shooting:** A goal is good if the *entire* volleyball passes through a hoop. It can go from front-to-back or back-to-front. (Rule `4.1.1`)

**Defensive Play:**

- **Tackling & Contact:** You can legally push (one-handed), wrap, and body block the opposing chaser with the volleyball.
    - **Front Contact Only:** All blocking, charging, and wrapping must be initiated from the front. Contact from behind is a foul. (Rule `6.1.9`)
    - **No Helpless Receivers:** You cannot make contact with a player who is in the air or in the process of catching the volleyball. Forceful contact is a Yellow Card, but **tackling a helpless receiver is a very dangerous play that results in a Red Card.** (Rules `6.1.7`, `6.1.13.G`)
- **Stealing:** You can strip or poke the ball loose from an opponent. You cannot grab their clothes or stick.

### **Keeper Zone: Your Fortress**

The Keeper Zone is the area from your endline to the keeper zone line. When you are the **Keeper** (green headband) inside this zone, you have special powers, but only until your team takes the volleyball out of the zone.

**Keeper Powers (While "Protected"):** (Rule `7.2.2.A`)
1.  **Dodgeball Immunity:** You cannot be knocked off stick by a beater's dodgeball. This protection is absolute as long as you are in your zone, regardless of whether you are on offense or defense.
2.  **Possession Immunity:** Once you have *sole possession* of the volleyball, opposing chasers cannot tackle you or try to steal the ball.

**Losing Your Powers:**
- Your immunity is temporary! As soon as you or a teammate carries or passes the volleyball **outside** the keeper zone, you lose all your special powers. You become a regular chaser until that offensive drive ends (your team scores, or the other team gets the ball). (Rule `7.2.2.B`)

**Restarting After a Goal:**
- After the other team scores, the volleyball is "dead." Your team's keeper must be the one to make it "live" again.
- The keeper can possess the ball anywhere in their half of the pitch to make it live. If another teammate touches it first, the keeper *must* possess it inside the keeper zone to restart play. (Rule `4.2.2`)
- **Keeper Delay:** You cannot substitute out after a goal until you have made the volleyball live.

### **Common Fouls & Violations for Chasers/Keepers**

Stay on the field by avoiding these common penalties.

| Foul/Violation                                     | Common Situation                                                                                                | Penalty                                       |
|----------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| **Illegal Contact from Behind**                    | Pushing, wrapping, or charging an opponent in the back.                                                         | **Yellow Card** (Standard Contact Penalty)     |
| **Contacting a Helpless Receiver**                 | Pushing a player who is jumping to catch a pass. *Tackling them is a Red Card.*                                 | **Yellow Card** or **Red Card**                |
| **Interacting with Dodgeballs**                    | Kicking or swatting a dodgeball. You can block a live dodgeball with the volleyball, but that's it.             | **Yellow Card** (Interposition Interference)   |
| **Delay of Game**                                  | Not advancing the volleyball with purpose; the keeper not moving the ball out of the zone quickly.              | **Blue Card + Volleyball Turnover**            |
| **Illegal Reset**                                  | Carrying or passing the ball back over midfield a second time in one possession.                                | **Volleyball Turnover**                        |
| **Goaltending**                                    | As a chaser, reaching through one of your own hoops from the back to block a shot. Only the keeper can do this. | **Good Goal** is awarded to the other team.    |
| **Playing while Off Stick**                        | Throwing the ball or tackling someone after being hit by a dodgeball or dismounting your stick.                 | **Blue Card** or **Yellow Card**               |
